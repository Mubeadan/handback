We believe lost things
deserve to be found

Handback started with a simple observation: losing something important is devastating — and yet most lost items end up unclaimed, not because no one found them, but because finder and loser had no way to connect.

Our story

The idea for Handback came from a real experience. A close friend lost all his personal belongings — his phone, his wallet, his ID. The experience was distressing not just because of the financial loss, but because of the helplessness. There was nowhere to go, no system to check, no way to find out if someone had picked up what he had lost.

That moment planted a question: why does this have to be so hard? In a city as connected as Nairobi — where almost everyone has a phone — why is there no simple platform for lost and found?

Handback is our answer. A free, simple, community-powered platform where finders and losers can find each other, communicate safely, and reunite people with the things that matter to them.

Privacy policy

Last updated: March 2026 · handback.co
We want you to understand exactly what we do with your information — in simple, clear language. This page explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and how we keep it safe. If you have any questions, you can always contact us directly.

1. Who we are

Handback is a free platform that helps people report lost and found items and connect with each other to get belongings returned. We are based in Nairobi, Kenya.

2. What information we collect

When you use Handback, we may collect:

  • Your name (first name only is fine) when you post a listing
  • Your contact details — phone number or email — only if you choose to share them
  • Photos and descriptions of the item you are reporting
  • The location where an item was lost or found
  • Messages you send to other users through our platform
  • Basic technical information like your device type and the browser you use

We never ask for your ID number, bank details, or any sensitive personal documents. If anyone claiming to be Handback asks for these, please report it to us immediately.

3. Why we collect this information

We only collect information that helps us run the platform:

  • Your name — so other users know who posted a listing
  • Your contact details — so a finder or loser can reach you when there is a match
  • Item photos and description — so people can identify their belongings
  • Location — so listings appear in the right area searches
  • Messages — so you can communicate safely through our platform

4. Who can see your information

Your listings — including the item description, photo, and general location — are visible to everyone who visits Handback. Your phone number or email is only shared if you choose the WhatsApp or email contact option.

We recommend using anonymous messaging to keep your personal contact details private until you feel comfortable sharing them.

5. We do not sell your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party — ever. Your data is used only to help reunite people with their belongings.

6. How we keep your data safe

We use secure servers and encrypted connections to protect your information. No system is 100% secure — please use a strong password and do not share your account details with anyone.

7. How long we keep your data

Your listing stays active for 90 days and is then automatically removed. You can delete your listing at any time from your account.

8. Your rights

You have the right to ask us what information we hold about you, correct any wrong information, or ask us to delete your data at any time.

9. Changes to this policy

If we make important changes, we will post a notice on the website. The date at the top always shows when it was last updated.

Questions about your privacy?

Email us at privacy@handback.co — we respond within 2 business days.

Terms of use

Last updated: March 2026 · handback.co
These are the rules for using Handback, written in plain language so they are easy to understand. By using this website, you agree to follow these rules.

1. What Handback is

Handback is a free community platform where people can post lost and found items and connect with each other. We help make the connection — but we are not responsible for what happens between users once they are in contact.

2. Who can use Handback

Anyone can use Handback for free. You must be at least 16 years old to create a listing or send messages. If you are younger, please ask a parent or guardian to help you.

3. What you are allowed to post

You may only post listings for genuinely lost or found items. Each listing must be honest and accurate.

Good listings help everyone. Please include a clear description, an accurate location, and a photo if you have one.

4. What you are not allowed to do

  • Post fake listings to scam or mislead people
  • Claim an item that does not belong to you
  • Ask for money or mobile money in exchange for returning an item
  • Post items that are illegal to own
  • Harass, threaten, or abuse other users
  • Use the platform to advertise products or services
  • Create multiple accounts to misuse the platform

Demanding payment to return someone's belongings is considered extortion and may be reported to the police. Handback will cooperate fully with any investigation.

5. Rewards

You may offer a reward — this is your choice. Handback does not handle or guarantee any reward. Any reward agreement is strictly between the two users involved.

6. Meeting in person

  • Meet in a busy public place — a shopping mall, petrol station, or bank entrance
  • Tell someone you trust where you are going
  • Do not go alone if you feel unsure
  • Verify the item belongs to the person before handing it over

7. Our responsibility

Handback provides the platform — we do not verify the identity of users or guarantee that any item will be returned. We are not responsible for any loss or dispute between users.

8. Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of Kenya.

Questions about these terms?

Email us at legal@handback.co — we respond within 2 business days.
